Introduction

Anolis is a well studied genus of iguanian reptiles that exists in many different ecomorphs, which are defined by groups of species with similar ecological niches and behaviors. Here, we aim to asses the relationship between hind limb length, snout vent length, and ecomorph using morphological and ecomorphological data from Hagey et al. (2017) and phylogenetic data from Poe (2017).

Methods

Phylogenetic generalized least squared models of log-transformed hind limb length versus snout vent length were constructed with and without the inclusion of ecomorph as a covariate of SVL, and using a Brownian or an Ornstein-Uhlenbeck model. Phylogenetic information was obtained from Poe (2017). An AicW operation was used to find the best-fitting model which was plotted along with a visualization of the residuals of this model.

  na.omit()

anole.log <- anole2%>%
  mutate_at(c("SVL", "HTotal","PH","ArbPD"),log)
### phylogenetic GLS models

#PGLS under BM, no ecomorph
pgls.BM1 <- gls(HTotal ~SVL, correlation = corBrownian(1,phy = anole.tree,form=~Species),data = anole.log, method = "ML")

#PGLS under BM, w ecomorph
pgls.BM2 <- gls(HTotal ~SVL * Ecomorph2, correlation = corBrownian(1,phy = anole.tree,form=~Species),data = anole.log, method = "ML")

#PGLS under OU, no ecomorph
pgls.OU1 <- gls(HTotal ~SVL, correlation = corMartins(0,phy = anole.tree,form=~Species),data = anole.log, method = "ML")

#PGLS under OU, w, ecomorph
pgls.OU2 <- gls(HTotal ~SVL * Ecomorph2, correlation = corMartins(0,phy = anole.tree,form=~Species),data = anole.log, method = "ML")
#AIC operations
anole.phylo.aic <- AICc(pgls.BM1,pgls.BM2,pgls.OU1,pgls.OU2)
anole.phylow.aicw <- aicw(anole.phylo.aic$AICc)

An AicW evaluation determined the Brownian motion model of character evolution including ecomorph as a covariate of snout vent length to be the best model for predicting the total hind limb length of anoles based on the snout vent length.

Discussion

The results show that the anole traits studied, snout vent length and hind limb length, evolved randomly within each species according to a Brownian model. The rejection of the model following Ornstein-Uhlenbeck evolution implies that these traits have not been pulled to a global optimum. The inclusion of ecomorph in the best fitting model of the phylogenetically corrected data proves that the ecomorph that each species of anole occupies does have an evolutionary effect on the hind limb length and snout vent length relationship.
